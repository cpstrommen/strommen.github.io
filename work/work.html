CNC Moldmaking for Injection Molding

I developed and implemented a workflow process that would allow for the sub-24 hour turnaround of injection molded parts through custom, in-house moldmaking processes. The file shown is the lower section of one of the more difficult molds I developed in this process, which would go on to full scale production shortly after.

In this project, I developed a process in which a small CNC mill could rapidly produce injection molds for new parts. Normally, 3D printed parts would be used to save on turnaround time (24 hours for 3D printed, 30 days minimum for injection molded). This allowed for customers to receive a more refined prototype on the same timeline a less developed prototype would normally be produced. In this project, I learned the basics of CNC and CAM along with small-scale injection molding.

Optimizing GPU Water Block Designs with Thermal Simulation

In my spare time, I noticed that the GPU chip on my graphics card was losing efficiency due to overheating, and I decided to design and build a custom water block to ensure optimal thermal transfer into the cooling loop. Through the use of Autodesk Fusion 360 thermal simulations, I was able to tweak and use self-optimization tools to design the optimal water block for the situation.

I was then able to manufacture this design using CNC technology and along with other parts of the loop I designed, construct a heat dissipation system for my Radeon HD6970 graphics card.
